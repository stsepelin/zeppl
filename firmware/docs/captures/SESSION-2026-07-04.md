# J1850 capture session — 2026-07-04 (first live bike sniff)

2009 VRSCF Muscle, RX-only sniffer through the proxy box, stock cluster
in place. First on-bike Stage 2 session.

> **RESOLVED (2026-07-04): the bus is standard VPW; there is no
> inversion.** The `RX_INVERT` flag was masking the 500 ns hardware
> glitch filter, which dropped the slow recessive (dominant->idle)
> falling edge. Three independent sources agree the bus idles LOW /
> dominant HIGH: (1) bare-bus DMM (ESP disconnected) reads idle ~0.3 V;
> (2) an invert-off raw dump on short leads shows `raw1 low 41 s` (idle
> LOW) then `raw2 HIGH 194 us` (SOF HIGH); (3) with the glitch filter
> off, decode is clean WITHOUT the invert flag — same-level=0, 0 bad
> CRC, the best capture yet. `RX_INVERT` and the low-side TX have both
> been removed. **TX is HIGH-side** (original `j1850_tx.svg`, PNP +
> 2N2907A). See the glitch-window sweep below.

## Root cause: the glitch filter ate the recessive edge (not inversion)

Pulse widths came back textbook VPW (64 / 128 / 200 us) but the decoder
saw 0 frames with correct polarity, and only decoded with
`CONFIG_VROD_J1850_RX_INVERT=y`. That was first read as bus inversion;
the bare-bus DMM (idle LOW) contradicted it. The real cause: the P4
hardware flex glitch filter delays the filtered edge relative to the
ISR's `gpio_get_level()` read, so the recessive falling edge is
recorded at the wrong level — it shows up as over-long HIGH pulses that
break frames and make idle gaps look HIGH. Under invert those long-HIGH
glitches became passive pulses that ended frames cleanly, which is why
the flag "worked."

**Glitch-window sweep (engine running, idle + throttle blips, 6 passes):**

| filter window | frames decoded | bad CRC |
|---|---|---|
| **0 ns (off)** | **125 / 208 / 213 per 9 s** | **0** |
| 400 ns | 0 | — |
| 800 ns | 0 | — |
| 1200 ns | 0 | — |
| 1600 ns | 0 | — |

Only the filter-off window decodes — a hard cliff, not a gradient (400 ns
breaks it as completely as 1600 ns), which is the signature of the
edge-vs-level-read delay, not slow-edge eating. The P4 flex filter also
tops out at 1600 ns (64-tick field on the 40 MHz XTAL; 800 ns on the
default 80 MHz clock), so the 1-5 us some notes imagined was never
reachable. **Tuned default: `CONFIG_VROD_J1850_GLITCH_NS=0`.** Real
noise immunity for the Phase 6 permanent build must come from RX
hysteresis (a comparator front end), not this filter. A candidate ISR
fix — infer the level by toggling instead of reading the pin — would
sidestep the delay race and let a filter coexist; deferred, not yet
tried.

## Ignition-on baseline (engine off) — what broadcasts

Bus is sparse at key-on; only these repeat, ~2 s cadence:

- `29 FE 40 ..` / `29 FE 60 ..`  — steady pair
- `68 FF 40 03 D8` / `68 FF 60 03 AD` — steady pair

None match our ECM-oriented decode table (`28 1B`, `48 29`, `A8 ..`) —
these are **IM-originated keep-alive**, the Stage 4 replay candidates.

One-shots seen at power-up: `48 92 40 2A`, `68 93 60 2A`, `29 81 60`,
`68 E0 B0`, and one `48 DA 40 39 00 70` (turn-signal header, value 00 =
off — matches the decode table).

Remember: this baseline has **neutral = engaged** and **beam bulb out**
baked in. Anything that reads those states is at its neutral/beam-off
value here.

## Decoded so far (engine off)

**Turn signals — `48 DA 40 39 XX`** (via TSSM, a bus node). `XX` is a
2-bit field, blinks between the value and `00`:

| XX | meaning | CRC byte |
|----|---------|----------|
| 00 | off | 70 |
| 02 | **left** | 4A |
| 01 | **right** | 6D |
| 03 | both / hazards (= 01\|02) | 57 |

Note: **left/right are swapped vs the HarleyDroid table** (it says
1=left, 2=right; this bike is 2=left, 1=right). Verified by test order
(left tested first → 02). Our CRC-8 matched every frame.

**Beam, neutral, oil are NOT on the bus.** Toggling high beam and
shifting out of neutral produced zero change in J1850 traffic. On the
V-Rod these are **discrete 12 V wires to the IM** — pin 2 White = high
beam, pin 10 Tan = neutral, pin 9 GN/Y = oil pressure — read locally by
the IM, not broadcast. We tap them directly via voltage dividers in
Phase 6. (Oil light ON at engine-off is normal: no oil pressure with
the engine stopped → pressure switch closed → lamp on; it extinguishes
once the engine builds pressure. Confirms the oil signal works.)

## Decoded — engine running (kill switch RUN woke the ECM)

All validated against live behaviour:

- **RPM — `28 1B 10 02 HH LL`**, `(HH<<8|LL)/4`. Confirmed: idle ~1300,
  tracked a clean sustained rev to **4125 rpm** and back, sampled ~15/s
  (66 ms interval, fast enough to drive a tach). Session max 4684.
- **Engine temp — `A8 49 10 10 XX`**, **raw byte = °C** (0x40=64°C →
  0x41=65°C, climbing as it warmed). Resolves the table's °C-vs-°F
  question: HarleyDroid's raw-°C is correct; the old plan's °F was wrong.
- **Gear — `A8 3B 10 03 XX`**, `00` = neutral (bits 1,3,7,15,31,63 →
  gears 1-6 per table; only neutral seen on the stand).
- **Check engine — `68 88 10 03`** = OFF (0x83 would be ON).
- **Fuel consumption — `A8 83 10 0A HH LL`** present (88 frames, ever-
  incrementing counter) — this is the tick stream for the fuel-computer
  fallback to the ultrasonic sender (see master plan Phase 6 caveat).
- **Speed — `48 29 10 02`** header confirmed present (reads 0 on the
  stand; the km/h-vs-mph divisor still needs a ride).

## IM keep-alive set (Stage 4 replay candidates)

connected — the messages the P4 must emit to impersonate the IM:

| Header | payload | interval |
|--------|---------|----------|
| `68 FF 40` | `03 D8` | ~2.0 s |
| `68 FF 60` | `03 AD` | ~2.0 s |
| `29 FE 40` | `01 64` | ~2.0 s |
| `29 FE 60` | `01 11` | ~2.0 s |

(Plus `C8 88 10`, `C8 89 60`, `E8 89 60`, `68 FF 10` — ECM/diagnostic
broadcasts, ~2-6 s. Confirm which are IM- vs ECM-originated by watching
what disappears when the stock cluster is unplugged — that's a Stage 4
